## **How to Install Pokégatchi on Your Wear OS Watch**

### **What You Need on Your PC**
*   **Android Studio:** The official software for building Android apps. If you don't have it, download it here: https://developer.android.com/studio
*   **ADB (Android Debug Bridge):** This is usually included with Android Studio. It's the command-line tool we use to talk to the watch.
*   **Your Watch's Charging Cable:** For the initial ADB connection, a direct USB connection is the most reliable method.

### **Part 1: Enable Developer Mode on Your Watch**
1.  On your watch, open the main **Settings** app (the gear icon ⚙️).
2.  Scroll down and tap on **System**.
3.  Inside System, tap on **About**.
4.  Find the entry labeled **Build number**.
5.  **Tap on "Build number" 7 times in a row, quickly.** You will see a small message counting you down. When it's done, it will say, "You are now a developer!"
6.  Go back to the main Settings menu. A new option, **Developer options**, will now be available. Tap on it.
7.  Inside Developer options, turn on both **ADB debugging** and **Debug over Wi-Fi**.

### **Part 2: Get the Code on Your PC**
1.  Open a terminal on your PC (like Git Bash or Command Prompt).
2.  Navigate to the folder where you keep your projects, for example: `cd C:\Users\George\Documents\GitHub`
3.  If you haven't already, clone the project from GitHub: `git clone https://github.com/bocaburger131/pokegatchi.git`
4.  Navigate into the project folder: `cd pokegatchi`
5.  Pull the very latest changes: `git pull`

### **Part 3: Connect Your PC to Your Watch**
1.  Connect your watch to your PC using its USB charging cable.
2.  On your watch screen, a prompt will appear asking "Allow debugging?". Check the box that says **"Always allow from this computer"** and then tap **"Allow"**.
3.  In the terminal on your PC, run this command to verify the connection: `adb devices`
4.  You should see your watch listed with "device" next to its name. This means you are connected.

### **Part 4: Build and Install the App with Android Studio**
1.  Open **Android Studio**.
2.  From the welcome screen, choose **"Open"** (or File > Open).
3.  Navigate to your project folder (e.g., `C:\Users\George\Documents\GitHub\pokegatchi`) and select the **`wearos-app`** folder inside it. Click "OK".
4.  The first time you open it, Android Studio will need a few minutes to set up the project (this is called "Gradle Sync"). Wait for all progress bars at the bottom to finish.
5.  At the top of the Android Studio window, you will see a dropdown menu that shows available devices. Your watch should be selected there.
6.  Click the green "Play" button (▶) to build and install the app on your watch.

That's it! The process will take a minute or two. Once it's done, the "Pokégatchi" app will appear in your watch's app list, ready to run.
